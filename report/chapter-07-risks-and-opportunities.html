Chapter 07 In progress · Outline

Risks and opportunities

Cybersecurity exposure, election integrity, education equity, and the upside cases — where South Carolina could lead nationally if the right pieces come together.

In progress

This chapter is being drafted.

The full chapter will be published in a subsequent edition. The outline below describes the topics the chapter will cover; if you are doing work in any of these areas and would like to be cited, contact SCAIO directly.

Outline

Risks the state should be planning for

  • Cybersecurity exposure. AI expands the attack surface for SC's public institutions — school districts, hospitals, municipal government, the courts, the ports — and lowers the cost of social engineering at scale. The state's existing posture, the SC Cyber Initiative, and the questions still open.
  • Election integrity. Generative AI's effect on voter information, deepfake risk, and the operational readiness of SC's State Election Commission.
  • Education equity. The risk that AI-augmented learning widens, rather than narrows, gaps between districts. What H.5253's parental-consent model means in practice.
  • Healthcare access. S.443 addresses one slice (insurer prior authorization), but rural-hospital deployment, AI-driven diagnostic triage, and patient-data governance remain to be worked through.
  • Workforce displacement and re-skilling speed. Drawing on Chapter 4 — what the state's training infrastructure can absorb, and where bottlenecks are forming.
  • Concentration risk in the ecosystem. A small number of large institutions account for most of SC's AI capacity. What that means for resilience and for smaller communities.

Opportunities where South Carolina could lead

  • Manufacturing AI. The state's existing strength in advanced manufacturing — BMW, Boeing, Volvo, Mercedes-Benz Vans, Michelin — combined with applied research at Clemson and USC, gives SC a credible claim to "applied industrial AI capital of the Southeast."
  • Defense and cyber AI. Charleston's existing defense, cyber, and intelligence community presence creates a distinctive opportunity that few other states can match.
  • Port and logistics AI. The Port of Charleston is one of the country's busiest container ports and has already begun digitizing operations; the next decade is when AI changes how ports run.
  • Rural broadband + agricultural AI. Clemson's precision-agriculture program and statewide broadband investment together could position SC's farms among the most data-rich in the country.
  • Health-system AI deployment. MUSC's Center for AI and Clemson–MUSC AI Hub, paired with the state's NCI-designated cancer center, is a credible national model for ethical clinical AI deployment.
  • State government AI literacy. The Center of Excellence and SC AI Strategy give the state an unusually structured starting point for AI in public service.
